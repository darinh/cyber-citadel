## Review Council Report: Cyber Citadel Video Series

Here's a breakdown of issues with the transcript, broken down by your requested categories. This is *high signal* – I’m not wasting words on fluff.

**A) BEGINNER GAPS (12 Terms/Concepts)**

1.  **Control:** Ep00, “Vega: A catalog…of controls you put to work.” - *A control is a safeguard or countermeasure used to avoid, transfer, or mitigate risk.*
2.  **Baseline:** Ep00, "NOVA: Hold on, where are the Low, Moderate, and High baselines?" – *A baseline is a pre-selected set of controls recommended for systems with different levels of risk.*
3.  **Framework:** Ep00, “VEGA: …how to read a control…where it all fits in the RMF.” - *A framework provides a structured process for managing information security and privacy risks.*
4.  **FISMA:** Ep00, "VEGA: A law named FISMA says agencies must protect their systems." – *FISMA (Federal Information Security Modernization Act) is a US federal law requiring government agencies to establish comprehensive information security programs.*
5.  **PII:** Ep03, “VEGA: …and privacy now stands beside security.” - *PII (Personally Identifiable Information) is any data that could potentially identify a specific individual.*
6.  **Authorization:** Ep11, "VEGA: The authorizing official grants the ATO…" – *Authorization is the official decision to allow a system to operate based on an assessment of its risks.*
7.  **Categorize:** Ep09, “VEGA: Before you pick defenses, you must know what you're defending…” - *To categorize means to determine the level of impact if the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of information is compromised.*
8.  **CIA Triad:** Ep09, “NOVA: Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability.” – *The CIA triad represents the three core goals of information security: keeping data secret, accurate, and accessible when needed.*
9. **Enhancements:** Ep00, "VEGA: Related controls point to its neighbors. Enhancements add strength." - *Enhancements are optional additions to a control that increase its effectiveness.*
10. **ATO (Authority To Operate):** Ep11, “VEGA: The authorizing official grants the ATO…” – *An ATO is formal permission for a system to run in production, granted after risk assessment and control implementation.*
11. **Vulnerability:** Ep05, "NULL: Every move I make... another tower turns my way." - *A vulnerability is a weakness in a system that could be exploited by a threat.* (Implied but never explained)
12. **Threat:** Ep00, “NULL: I am the Null…” – *A threat is anything that has the potential to cause harm to a system or organization.* (Also implied.)

**B) MISSING FOUNDATIONS (5 Items)**

1.  **What *is* an Information System?**: Completely absent. Needs a 20-second explanation of what constitutes an "information system" in the federal context (hardware, software, data, people, processes).
2.  **Who is NIST?:** Absent. A brief intro to NIST as a non-regulatory agency that develops standards and guidelines. ("NIST is like the research lab for US cybersecurity.")
3.  **What *is* a Control? (Everyday Example):** Rushed. The analogy of "menu items" isn't strong enough. Needs an example: “Like locking your doors at night – that’s a control to protect against theft.”
4.  **Law vs. Standard vs. Framework:** Absent. A clear distinction is needed. (FISMA = Law, 800-53 = Standard, RMF = Framework).
5.  **Threat vs. Vulnerability vs. Risk:** Implicit but never explicitly explained. Needs a quick visual/analogy: “A vulnerability is an unlocked door, the threat is a burglar, and risk is how likely they are to break in.”

**C) PACING/CLARITY (Examples)**

*   **Ep01:** The jump from "three families" to "six layers" is confusing. Needs more explanation of *why* those groupings exist and what the purpose is.
*   **Ep04:**  The High Council section feels rushed. Four families are introduced quickly, without enough time to connect them back to the RMF steps.
*   **Ep05:** The Forge section tries to cover too much (acquisition *and* maintenance *and* supply chain) in one episode. Should be split into two.
*   **Ep10:** Tailoring with overlays is glossed over. Needs a concrete example of how an overlay works.
* **Ep11:** Assessment, Authorization and Continuous Monitoring are crammed together - this needs breathing room to explain the ongoing nature of security.

**D) ENGAGEMENT IDEAS (6 Concrete Ideas – Motion Graphics + Avatar Portraits Only)**

1.  **Control Cards:** When a control is mentioned, display a visually distinct card with its ID and title on screen.
2.  **Threat Visualization:** During Null's lines, show subtle visual glitches or distortions in the "Citadel" backdrop to represent his attacks.
3.  **Layered Map:** As each layer of defense is introduced, highlight it on an animated map of the Citadel.
4.  **Avatar Reactions:** Use Nova’s avatar to visually express confusion or understanding during Vega's explanations. (e.g., a lightbulb icon above her head)
5.  **RMF Flowchart:** A dynamic flowchart illustrating the RMF steps, updating as the series progresses.
6.  **Control "Power-Ups":** When an enhancement is discussed, show a brief visual effect ("powering up") on the corresponding control card. (e.g., a glowing border)

*   **VEGA:** “AC-6, Least Privilege.” *(Display AC-6 Control Card)*

**E) ACCURACY PITFALLS (Top 5)**

1.  **(HIGH) Ep04, RA-3 is presented as *the* basis for every choice.** While important, it's the *initial* risk assessment. Continuous monitoring and changing threats require ongoing reassessment.
2.  **(HIGH) Ep06, Implying backups are solely for ransomware recovery.** Backups protect against all data loss scenarios (hardware failure, natural disasters, etc.). Needs broader context.
3.  **(MEDIUM) Ep05, SR section implies component authenticity is *only* about counterfeits.** It’s also about tampering and supply chain integrity issues beyond just fakes.
4.  **(MEDIUM) Ep10, Overly simplistic view of tailoring.** Tailoring isn't just "adding" overlays; it involves removing unnecessary controls based on risk assessment.
5.  **(LOW) Ep07, Presenting the RMF as a linear process.** While the diagram is useful, emphasize that it’s *iterative* and continuous monitoring feeds back into preparation/categorization.
