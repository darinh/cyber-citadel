**B) MISSING FOUNDATIONS – 5 things a brand‑new learner needs but are absent or rushed**

1. **What is an information system?** – A brief definition (hardware + software that processes data).  
2. **Who is NIST and why its documents matter?** – Explain NIST as the U.S. standards body and its role in federal IT security.  
3. **Law vs. standard vs. framework** – Clarify that FISMA is law, 800‑53 is a standard (what to do), RMF is a framework (when to do it).  
4. **Everyday example of a control** – e.g., “Password expiration every 90 days” as a concrete control.  
5. **Threat vs. vulnerability vs. risk** – Simple definitions and how they interrelate.

**E) ACCURACY PITFALLS – statements that mis‑state or risk confusion**

| # | Mis‑statement (quoted) | Why it’s wrong / risky | Suggested fix |
|---|------------------------|------------------------|---------------|
|1| ep09: “The catalog has a control for exactly this: RA‑2.” | RA‑2 is *Risk Assessment Process*, not categorization. | Re‑write as “RA‑3 is the formal risk assessment; RA‑2 describes the process.” |
|2| ep04: “RA‑3 is the formal risk assessment.” | Correct in spirit, but RA‑3 is actually *Risk Assessment* (the output), whereas RA‑2 is the *process*. | Clarify both roles. |
|3| ep05: “SA‑22 says replace it, or arrange alternate support.” | SA‑22 is *System Acquisition*, not maintenance; the description fits MA‑2 or SA‑21. | Replace with correct control (e.g., “MA‑2 requires scheduled maintenance and patching”). |
|4| ep07: “All 20 + RMF” – implying 20 controls plus RMF. | The 20 families are part of the catalog; RMF is a separate process, not an extra set of controls. | Rephrase as “The 20 control families embedded in the RMF.” |
|5| ep11: “CA‑2 tests the controls, with procedures from companion doc 800‑53A.” | CA‑2 is *Security Assessments*; it includes testing but also review and interview. | Expand to mention all three methods (examine, interview, test). |

**Top five fixes (overall importance)**

1. Correct RA‑2/RA‑3 labeling to avoid confusion about risk assessment vs. process.  
2. Clarify that SA‑22 is acquisition; use MA‑2 for maintenance/patching.  
3. Re‑state the 20 families as part of the catalog, not an extra RMF set.  
4. Expand CA‑2 description to include all three assessment methods.  
5. Add a brief definition of “information system” early in the course.

These corrections preserve technical accuracy while keeping the narrative flow intact.
